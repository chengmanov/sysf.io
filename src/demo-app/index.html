What are kernels?

Kernels are the low-level GPU programs that do the model's actual math — the 1-bit matrix multiplies, linear attention and normalizations behind every token. Each one is a WGSL compute shader, compiled by your browser for this GPU.

  • Written by AI agents. Every kernel in this list was authored by Fable 5 and GPT 5.6 Sol — then tested for correctness and benchmarked on the shapes it serves before earning its place.
  • 1-bit weights, full-speed math. Packed binary weights are decoded inside the kernel itself — what you read below is the real inference path.
  • Fully local. These shaders — and your conversation — never leave this machine.
